Kaveh Jahanshahi

I am currently a PhD student at the Department of Architecture, University of Cambridge. My PhD research examines how best to structure passenger trips distribution models for improving the travel demand forecasting. I am also actively involved in research into urban modelling and planning within the Department.

Before starting my PhD, I was working as a senior transport modeller for WSP consultancy for more than 6 years with responsibility for technical leading and financial management of several innovative projects, including building DfT’s National Transport Model (NTM) , APRIL, a sophisticated transport model of London to allow the testing of various road pricing, cordon charging and area licensing options , and updating and supporting TEMPRO, the DfT’s online transport planning resource. I have extensive experience in Transport and land use modelling and planning , travel demand analysis, freight modelling and multi-modal modelling and planning policy impact studies for urban developments of varying scale.
